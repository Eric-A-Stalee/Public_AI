March 11, 2026 — The Druckenmiller View

> The U.S. economy is caught between competing forces. Growth data is mixed — GDP disappointed in Q4 2025 at 1.4% but ISM manufacturing and services are both expanding. The labor market is the weakest link, with February showing outright job losses and unemployment rising to 4.4%.

> Inflation is the Fed's primary constraint. CPI is at 2.4% but core PCE is estimated at 3.1% — 110bps above target. The Iran conflict has pushed oil to ~$90+/bbl, threatening to re-accelerate energy inflation. Every $10/bbl sustained adds ~5bps to core PCE.

> The Fed is stuck. Rates at 3.50-3.75% are too restrictive for a weakening labor market but too loose given persistent inflation. Markets have pushed rate cut expectations to September, with Kevin Warsh expected to take over as Fed Chair around that time.

> The Druckenmiller framework: Gold at ATHs signals monetary/fiscal stress. The yield curve has re-steepened (2s10s +56bps), which historically precedes economic slowdown. M2 at new highs provides a liquidity floor but isn't accelerating. The resolution depends entirely on oil/Iran and whether AI displacement becomes a structural headwind for employment.
